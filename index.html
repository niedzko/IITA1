Personal Information

I am a 47-year-old father of 5 kids from a blended family. Born in Poland, my family migrated to Australia in 1982 as political refugees in order to escape the communist regime I was born into. As a teenager I discovered the guitar and planned to spend the rest of my life as a rock star. This plan was somewhat thwarted when I first become a father at age 23. Whilst this was a great moment in my life, it meant getting a ‘real job’. I was lucky to earn a role in Telstra in 1999 and, aside from a brief experiment with a competitor company, have been there ever since.

Being a short-sighted teenager, I did not complete my engineering degree, so I feel very fortunate to have had a success career with a big company – and grateful that it has exposed me to the world of IT.

I have since discovered that continual learning is one of my personal values, hence my decision to return to university. I aim to enjoy the process of learning again and prove to myself that I can indeed work towards successful completion of formal study.

Outside of work, I have discovered the pleasures of running and compete in half-marathons. I still dream of being a professional musician but am content with keeping it just that – a dream.

Interest in IT

Before I was a teenager, I was the one called in to programme the VCR (mum couldn’t miss Days of Our Lives!) and could not understand why my parents didn’t know how – I simply thought the process was quite logical.

I commence my work at Telstra in an ordinary data entry role, servicing the nations internet service providers (ISP’s). This was in the days of dial-up internet which meant a local ISP provided the cheapest form of connectivity as it was a local call. I happened to sit next to a colleague by the name of Joe who was part of our assurance team, providing first-in testing for our ISP help desk.

In moments of quiet, Joe showed me what an IP address was and taught me that it could be ‘pinged’! I was fascinated and, through his continued mentoring, eventually moved to the assurance team and learnt about the wonderful world of IP networking.

I have since been in and around IT for almost my entire career yet, apart from vendor certifications, have no formal training or tertiary education. I have never been able to build, design or deploy any form of technology, but am fascinated by the process. I current work in a business develop role selling workplace technology and have had what I consider to be a very successful career. Yet I often look to the ‘doers’ in my field, the engineers and consultants that actually deliver what I have sold, and feel admiration for their knowledge and abilities.
